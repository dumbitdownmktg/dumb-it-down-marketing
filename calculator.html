What is cost per lead?

It's how much you spend in marketing dollars to get one person to raise their hand and say "I'm interested." If you spent $500 on Facebook ads and got 20 leads, your cost per lead is $25.

Why does it matter?

Because without knowing this number, you're flying blind. Some channels are cheap per lead but those leads never buy. Others cost more but close at 80%. The goal is knowing which dollar is working hardest for you.

What's a "good" number?

Depends entirely on what a client is worth to you. A $25 lead cost is great if your average client pays you $2,000. It's a disaster if your average sale is $40. Context is everything.
